## Key Measurement Tables by Guideline

### LV Diastolic Function (ASE 2025)
**Grade I (Impaired Relaxation, Normal Filling Pressure)**
- E/A < 0.8, E < 50 cm/s
- Average E/e' ≤ 14
- TR velocity ≤ 2.8 m/s
- LAVI ≤ 34 mL/m²

**Grade II (Pseudonormal)**
- E/A 0.8–2.0
- Average E/e' 9–14 (indeterminate) or ≥ 15 (elevated LAP)
- TR velocity 2.9–3.4 m/s
- LAVI 35–40 mL/m²

**Grade III (Restrictive)**
- E/A > 2.0, E > 50 cm/s
- Average E/e' ≥ 15
- TR velocity > 3.4 m/s
- LAVI > 40 mL/m²

**HFpEF Diagnosis (H2FPEF Score)**
- Heavy (BMI > 30): 2 pts
- Hypertensive (≥2 antihypertensives): 1 pt
- Atrial Fibrillation: 3 pts
- Pulmonary Hypertension (PASP > 35): 1 pt
- Elder (age > 60): 1 pt
- Filling Pressure (E/e' > 9): 1 pt

### Right Heart & PH (ASE 2025)
**RV Size (PLAX)**
- Normal: RVOT prox < 33 mm, RVOT distal < 27 mm
- Mildly dilated: 33–38 mm / 27–33 mm
- Moderately dilated: 38–43 mm / 33–38 mm
- Severely dilated: > 43 mm / > 38 mm

**RV Function**
- TAPSE: Normal ≥ 17 mm; Mildly reduced 12–16 mm; Severely reduced < 12 mm
- S' (TDI): Normal ≥ 9.5 cm/s; Reduced < 9.5 cm/s
- FAC: Normal ≥ 35%; Mildly reduced 25–34%; Severely reduced < 25%
- RV GLS: Normal ≤ -20%; Mildly reduced -15 to -19%; Severely reduced > -15%
- RIMP (PW): Normal ≤ 0.40; Abnormal > 0.40
- RIMP (TDI): Normal ≤ 0.54; Abnormal > 0.54

**Pulmonary Hypertension**
- TR Vmax > 3.4 m/s → High probability PH
- TR Vmax 2.9–3.4 m/s + additional signs → Intermediate probability
- TR Vmax ≤ 2.8 m/s + no additional signs → Low probability
- PASP = 4 × (TR Vmax)² + RAP

**RA Pressure Estimation (IVC)**
- IVC ≤ 21 mm + collapsibility > 50%: RAP 3 mmHg (range 0–5)
- IVC > 21 mm + collapsibility < 50%: RAP 15 mmHg (range 10–20)
- Indeterminate: RAP 8 mmHg (range 5–10)

### Chamber Quantification (ASE 2015)
**LV Linear Dimensions (2D guided M-mode)**
- LVIDd: Men 42–58 mm; Women 38–52 mm
- LVIDs: Men 25–40 mm; Women 22–35 mm
- IVSd: Men 6–10 mm; Women 6–9 mm
- LVPWd: Men 6–10 mm; Women 6–9 mm

**LV Volumes (Biplane Simpson)**
- LVEDV: Men 62–150 mL; Women 46–106 mL
- LVESV: Men 21–61 mL; Women 14–42 mL
- LVEF: Normal ≥ 52% (men), ≥ 54% (women)
- Mildly reduced: 41–51% (men), 41–53% (women)
- Moderately reduced: 30–40%
- Severely reduced: < 30%

**LA Volume**
- LAVI: Normal ≤ 34 mL/m²; Mildly enlarged 35–41; Moderately 42–48; Severely ≥ 49

**Aortic Root**
- Aortic root (sinuses): Men ≤ 40 mm; Women ≤ 36 mm
- Ascending aorta: Men ≤ 40 mm; Women ≤ 37 mm

### Valvular Disease
**Aortic Stenosis Severity**
- Severe: Vmax ≥ 4 m/s, mean PG ≥ 40 mmHg, AVA ≤ 1.0 cm²
- Moderate: Vmax 3–3.9 m/s, mean PG 20–39 mmHg, AVA 1.0–1.5 cm²
- Mild: Vmax 2–2.9 m/s, mean PG < 20 mmHg, AVA > 1.5 cm²
- Low-flow Low-gradient Severe AS: AVA ≤ 1.0 cm², Vmax < 4 m/s, EF < 50%

**Mitral Regurgitation (ASE 2017)**
- Severe: EROA ≥ 0.40 cm² (primary), ≥ 0.20 cm² (secondary); RVol ≥ 60 mL (primary), ≥ 30 mL (secondary); RF ≥ 50%
- Moderate: EROA 0.20–0.39 cm²; RVol 30–59 mL; RF 30–49%
- Mild: EROA < 0.20 cm²; RVol < 30 mL; RF < 30%

**Tricuspid Regurgitation**
- Severe: EROA ≥ 0.40 cm²; RVol ≥ 45 mL; VC ≥ 7 mm
- Moderate: EROA 0.20–0.39 cm²; VC 3–6.9 mm
- Mild: EROA < 0.20 cm²; VC < 3 mm

**Aortic Regurgitation**
- Severe: EROA ≥ 0.30 cm²; RVol ≥ 60 mL; RF ≥ 50%; PHT < 200 ms
- Moderate: EROA 0.10–0.29 cm²; RVol 30–59 mL; RF 30–49%
- Mild: EROA < 0.10 cm²; RVol < 30 mL; RF < 30%

### Strain Imaging (ASE 2025)
**LV Global Longitudinal Strain (GLS)**
- Normal: ≤ -20% (more negative = better)
- Mildly reduced: -15% to -19%
- Moderately reduced: -10% to -14%
- Severely reduced: > -10% (less negative)
- Cardiotoxicity threshold: > 15% relative reduction from baseline

**RV Free Wall Strain**
- Normal: ≤ -20%
- Reduced: > -20%

### Fetal Echocardiography (ASE 2023)
**Normal Fetal Cardiac Measurements (20 weeks)**
- Cardiac axis: 45° ± 20°
- Cardiothoracic ratio: < 0.35
- RV/LV ratio: approximately 1:1
- MPI (Tei Index): LV ≤ 0.53; RV ≤ 0.55

**Fetal Biometry Z-scores**
- Z-score < -2: consider pathological
- Z-score -2 to +2: normal range
- Z-score > +2: consider pathological

### POCUS (ASE 2024)
**IVC Assessment**
- IVC diameter < 2.1 cm + collapsibility > 50%: Low RAP (0–5 mmHg)
- IVC diameter > 2.1 cm + collapsibility < 50%: High RAP (10–20 mmHg)

**LV Function (POCUS)**
- Visual EF estimation: Normal ≥ 50%; Reduced < 50%
- E-point septal separation (EPSS): Normal < 7 mm; Reduced EF > 7 mm

**Pleural/Pericardial Effusion**
- Small pericardial effusion: < 10 mm
- Moderate: 10–20 mm
- Large: > 20 mm
- Tamponade signs: RA collapse > 1/3 systole, RV diastolic collapse, IVC plethora, respiratory variation > 25% (mitral), > 40% (tricuspid)
